Title: Footnotes and Special Character Handling in Python
URL: https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/introduction.html
Summary: This section clarifies the precedence of operators in Python, highlighting how `-3**2` evaluates to `-9` due to the precedence of `**`. It also explains the behavior of single and double quotes in Python, noting that special characters like `
` function the same in both, while escaping rules differ.
---

```



Footnotes
[[1](https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/introduction.html#id1)]
Since `**` has higher precedence than `-`, `-3**2` will be interpreted as `-(3**2)` and thus result in `-9`. To avoid this and get `9`, you can use `(-3)**2`.
[[2](https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/introduction.html#id2)]
Unlike other languages, special characters such as `\n` have the same meaning with both single (`'...'`) and double (`"..."`) quotes. The only difference between the two is that within single quotes you don’t need to escape `"` (but you have to escape `\'`) and vice versa.
